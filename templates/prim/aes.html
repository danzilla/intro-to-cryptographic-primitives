Symetric Encryption

Symmetric encryption used to conceal the contents of blocks or streams of data of any size, including messages, files, encryption keys, and passwords.

AES Advanced Encryption Standard

AES is a block cipher. The plaintext is broken up into 128 byte chunks which are encrypted seperately. Ideally, the encrypted ciphertext of each block is XORed with the plaintext of the next one.

IV - Initialization vector is a block of bits that is used by several modes to randomize the encryption. Sort of works like adding salt value to a Hash function to make "uniqe"

Padding is to add null bytes to the plaintext to bring its length up to a multiple of the block size. Mostly used for ECB and CBC modes

Modes

CFB - Cipher Feedback
CBC - Cipher Blocking Chaining
OFB - Output Feedback
