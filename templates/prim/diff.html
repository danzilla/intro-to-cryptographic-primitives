Diffie-Hellman

After implementing this primitive, two Users mutually agree on a Prime Number that will be shared together and generator in clear text. Then, both users will generate a random number to be used as a private key and will not divulge this information to any other party. Both users should memorize their private key as well.

So at this stage, the only information a third party will have are the Prime Number and Generator. Now both users will have a Public Key that is computed from their respective secret key and prime number. Both public keys will be then shared to each other over a public domain. So at this stage, a third party will get both users public key.

After sharing both public keys to each other, both users will receive the same shared key that is computed from their respective secret keys, prime numbers and prime number. Now both users can exchange information with each other with their shared key, without a third party interfering.
